Revision: Units 13-20

Based on Essential Grammar in Use (Murphy, 4th ed.) — elementary level. Read each unit before the quiz.

Unit 13 — I was doing (Past Continuous)

was / were + verb-ing = an action that was in progress at a particular time in the past.

SubjectForm
I / he / she / itwas working
we / you / theywere working
I was studying at 8 o'clock. They were playing football. It was raining.
Use the past continuous for actions in progress, not completed actions.

Unit 14 — Past Continuous & Past Simple

Use Past Continuous for the background action and Past Simple for the shorter action that happened during it.

Past ContinuousPast Simple
I was watching TVwhen the phone rang.
They were walkingwhen it started to rain.
While I was cooking, my friend arrived.

Unit 15 — Present Perfect (1)

have / has + past participle

Use the present perfect for actions that happened in the past but are connected to the present.

VerbPast Participle
gogone
seeseen
dodone
writewritten
I've lost my keys. She's finished her homework.

Unit 16 — Present Perfect (just / already / yet)

Use these common words with the present perfect.

WordMeaning
justa short time ago
alreadyearlier than expected
yetuntil now (negative/questions)
I've just eaten. She has already left. Have you finished yet?

Unit 17 — Have you ever...?

Use ever to ask about life experiences and never for negative experiences.

Have you ever been to Bagan? I've never flown in a helicopter.

Short answers: Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.

Do not use a specific past time (yesterday, last week) with ever.

Unit 18 — How long have you...?

Use the present perfect to talk about something that started in the past and continues now.

I've lived here for five years. She has worked here since 2022.
QuestionAnswer
How long have you lived here?For five years.
How long has he worked here?Since January.

Unit 19 — for / since / ago

WordUse
fora period of time (for two years)
sincea starting point (since Monday)
agobefore now (two years ago)
I've lived here for three years. I've lived here since 2022. I came here three years ago.

Unit 20 — Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Present PerfectPast Simple
No finished timeFinished time
I've seen that film.I saw it yesterday.
Have you ever been there?Did you go last year?
I've finished my homework. I finished it last night.
Use Present Perfect with ever, never, just, already, yet, for, since.
Use Past Simple with yesterday, last week, in 2024, two days ago.
